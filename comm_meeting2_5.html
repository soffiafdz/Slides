Background and Related Work

Alzheimer's Disease

Cognitive decline in this project relates specifically to MCI as in the context of it being a preliminary stage of AD.
Alzheimer’s disease (AD) is a chronic, progressive and ultimately fatal neurological disorder in which the brain progressively and irreversibly deteriorates, affecting the quality of life of both the patient their close family members and caregivers.

Pathological Nature

The neuropathology of AD is characterized by:
(1) deposition and accumulation of β-amyloid (Aβ) peptides, or amyloid/senile plaques between the neuronal extracellular space,
-Starts basal portions of cortex on the medial-temporal-lobe;
-Asocciation areas;
-All areas including sensory and motor core fields.
(2) formation of neuronal intracellular neurofibrillary tangles of hyperphosphorylated tau protein,
-Braak Stages I,II: transentorhinal
-Braak Stages III,IV: limbic
-Braak Stages V,VI: isocortical
(3) neuronal loss, and (4) neuroinflammation (Miziak et al., 2021).

Biological affliction precedes symptoms by decades

The first accumulation of Aꞵ in the brain can occur one to two decades before the first appearance of clinical symptoms (Pluta et al., 2020). Both CSF/PET amyloid and postmortem studies suggest that a substantial proportion of cognitively normal subjects present evidence of Aβ accumulation. The exact proportion is dependent on age and genetic background of the study cohort, but ranges from approximately 20 to 40% (Sperling et al., 2011).
1) (CSF) and plasma Aβ42/Aβ40 ratio.
2) CSF and plasma tau increases, as a neuronal response to the AB changes.
3) AB PET turns positive.
4) Microglia react and secrete soluble triggering receptor expressed on myeloid cells 2 (sTREM2), maximum in MCI stage declines in dementia phase.
5) CSF neurogranin (Ng): early marker of synaptic dysfunction; increases w/amyloid PET.

7) Astrocytic biomarkers: YKL-40 and glial fibrillary acidic protein [GFAP].

Hypotheses

Hypotheses

  1. The calculation of HVR, can be reliably done using automatic methods of segmentation.
  2. HVR will show stronger relationships with memory and age than conventional HC volume measures in a large cohort of healthy controls (UKBB).
  3. HVR will show better prediction capabilities of cognitive decline than other HC volume methods in large cohorts of patients with MCI and mild AD (ADNI, AIBL, HABS).
  4. There will be significant differences in HVR spatiotemporal curves between:
    • Dementia-progressors v No-cognitive-decline;
    • Amyloid+ v Amyloid-;
    • APOE4+ v APOE4-.

Results

  • Raw MRI data (N=99; 9 raters)
    Agreement with experienced rater: 59-86% (mean 77%)
    Qrater vs Traditional QC: comparable speeds
  • Linear-registered MRI data (N=99; 8 raters)
    Agreement with experienced rater: 73-92% (mean 84.75%)
    Qrater vs Traditional QC: 2-3x faster
  • Skull extracted images (N=1,746; 2 raters)
    Kappa value of 0.83 as inter-rater agreement
    Qrater vs Traditional QC: 20x faster
    • Even with the high speeds, we got good consistency between raters

    Lreg: expert - 99 v 43 in 36 min; trainee - 99 v 30 in 37 min

Qrater, an open-source web-application free to use and available to download to facilitate Quality Control evaluation of any kind of imaging task in a simple to use and collaborative way. A tool for the automatic tissue segmentation of the hippocampus and its surrounding temporal horn of the lateral ventricle. Given the role of the hippocampus in healthy and pathological aging, this tool will prove useful for the studies of other types of neurodegenerative diseases. A new standardized metric for hippocampal volume proved to be more capable of capturing its atrophy. Normative centile tables stratified by sex of raw hippocampal volume and HVR that will provide valuable information regarding normal variations of these measures. The finding of the distinct possible disease progression trajectories in AD and a set of prediction models able to identify the risk of a new patient belonging to one of these classes.
