Background and Related Work

Alzheimer's Disease

High Prevalence

High economic cost

Pathological Nature

Biological affliction precedes symptoms by decades

Hippocampus

  • Viable proxy for structural and functional integrity
  • High variability in volume across populations

Hippocampal-to-Ventricle Ratio

  • Ventricular enlargement is a marker of brain atrophy
  • Schoemaker, et al (2019) used manual segmentation to compute the ratio between prehippocampal CSF and hippocampal volume
  • It showed significantly stronger negative associations with age and was a better predictor of memory compared to standard hippocampal volume

Hypotheses

Hypotheses

  1. The calculation of HVR, can be reliably done using automatic methods of segmentation.
  2. HVR will show stronger relationships with memory and age than conventional HC volume measures in a large cohort of healthy controls (UKBB).
  3. HVR will show better prediction capabilities of cognitive decline than other HC volume methods in large cohorts of patients with MCI and mild AD (ADNI, AIBL, HABS).
  4. There will be significant differences in HVR spatiotemporal curves between:
    • Dementia-progressors v No-cognitive-decline;
    • Amyloid+ v Amyloid-;
    • APOE4+ v APOE4-.

AIM 1

AIM 2

Compare different segmentatation techniques and select the most appropriate one for automatic calculation of HVR

AIM 2

  • Available labels from Schoemaker, et al.
  • Compare the performance of different segmentation techniques:
    • Multi-atlas label fusion; Collins & Pruessner, 2010;
    • Non-local Patch-based segmentation; Coupé, et al., 2011
    • Convolutional Neural Network; Novosad, et al., 2020

Multi-atlas Label Fusion

Multi-atlas Label Fusion

  • Monte Carlo Cross-Validation (MCCV)
  • Template library: n=20

Non-local Patch-based

Non-local Patch-based

  • Monte Carlo Cross-Validation (MCCV)
  • Training library: n=20
  • Patch: 7x7x7 voxels
  • Search box: 15x15x15 voxels

3D Fully Convolutional Network

3D Fully Convolutional Network

  • Linear-registration-derived spatial priors
  • Data augmentation: random elastic deformations
  • Regularization: Dropout and early-stopping
  • Parameter optimization: RMSProp
  • Loss: Categorical cross-entropy

Performance comparison

  • Dice coefficient
  • Modified Hausdorff distance
