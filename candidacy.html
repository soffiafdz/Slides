Background and Related Work

Alzheimer's Disease

High Prevalence

High economic cost

Alzheimer’s disease (AD) is a chronic, progressive and ultimately fatal neurological disorder in which the brain progressively and irreversibly deteriorates, affecting the quality of life of both the patient and their close family members.

Pathological Nature

The neuropathology of AD is characterized by: (1) deposition and accumulation of β-amyloid (Aβ) peptides, or amyloid plaques between the neuronal extracellular space, (2) formation of neuronal intracellular neurofibrillary tangles of hyperphosphorylated tau protein, (3) neuronal loss, and (4) neuroinflammation (Miziak et al., 2021).

Biological affliction precedes symptoms by decades

The first accumulation of Aꞵ in the brain can occur one to two decades before the first appearance of clinical symptoms (Pluta et al., 2020). Both CSF and PET amyloid imaging and postmortem studies suggest that a substantial proportion of cognitively normal subjects present evidence of Aβ accumulation. The exact proportion is dependent on age and genetic background of the study cohort, but ranges from approximately 20 to 40% (Sperling et al., 2011).

Hypotheses

Hypotheses

  1. The calculation of HVR, can be reliably done using automatic methods of segmentation.
  2. HVR will show stronger relationships with memory and age than conventional HC volume measures in a large cohort of healthy controls (UKBB).
  3. HVR will show better prediction capabilities of cognitive decline than other HC volume methods in large cohorts of patients with MCI and mild AD (ADNI, AIBL, HABS).

Multi-atlas Label Fusion

  • Monte Carlo Cross-Validation (MCCV): 5 rounds
  • Template library: n=20

Non-local Patch-based

  • MCCV: 5 rounds
  • Training library: n=20
  • Patch size: 7x7x7 voxels
  • Search box: 15x15x15 voxels

3D Fully Convolutional Network

3D Fully Convolutional Network

  • Linear-registration-derived spatial priors
  • Data augmentation: random elastic deformations
  • Regularization: Dropout and early-stopping
  • Parameter optimization: RMSProp
  • Loss: Categorical cross-entropy
  • Independent test set: 20% hold-out
We therefore make use of whole spatial coordinate patches: given an input patch centered on spatial coordinate (x,y,z) in image space, we extract three additional patches (with the same spatial dimensions as the input patch) centered on spatial coordinate (x,y,z) from each of three “coordinate images.” For example, for the x-coordinate image, the value at spatial coordinate (x,y,z)is simply x, and similar for the y- and z-coordinate images. The three spatial coordinate patches are then concatenated with the image intensity patch, forming a multichannel input

A tool for the automatic tissue segmentation of the hippocampus and its surrounding temporal horn of the lateral ventricle. Given the role of the hippocampus in healthy and pathological aging, this tool will prove useful for the studies of other types of neurodegenerative diseases.
